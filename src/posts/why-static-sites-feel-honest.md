Static sites are nice because they do not pretend to be a distributed system.

You write the page, ship the page, and the browser reads the page. Very rude of it to be so simple.

![Reach me here](/image/reach-me-here.svg)

## The whole trick

- Keep the build boring.
- Keep the content close to the repo.
- Keep the deployment understandable at 02:13.

> A page that can be copied as files has fewer places to lie.

The nice part: markdown still gives links like [Vite](https://vite.dev), `inline code`, **strong opinions**, and *small jokes* without asking for a database.
